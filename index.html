PharmaFriend

Over-the-counter drugs are easily accessible to the consumer. They do not require a prescription to purchase, thus presenting the concern that individuals may purchase medication detrimental to their drug plan and health. Pharmafriend is a chat-bot based android app that prevents this from happening. The app accesses drug data from REST APIs to assess the risks of the medication. It communicates with the user via a chat-bot that can recognize and respond to social cues. This bot was created by developing a language-understanding model using Microsoft LUIS.

XenoClassify

Xenoclassify is an open-source command-line tool that classifies sequencing data from xenograft samples with 96% accuracy. The classification algorithm was created by performing statistical analysis on data known to be from the host or graft species to identify features characteristic of host or graft DNA. The tool is able to classify DNA from a xenograft sample into four classes: host, graft, both, or neither, thus allowing bioinformaticians to carry out analysis on the DNA of choice. Code optimizations such as multiprocessing and chunking were employed to efficiently handle large BAM files (100 GB+).

Click here to visit the project's Github repository.

Freezing of Gait

In my first year of engineering, I designed a wearable device that detects freezing of gait in Parkinson’s patients with a 94% success rate. The brace was fashioned from a number of hardware, software, and structural components. The embedded system used an Arduino, IMU, laser, and motor fastened to an ankle cast. The detection algorithm identifies frequency changes in acceleration to administer physical and visual cues to reinitiate normal walking.

Cell Biology Publication

One characteristic that is present in every cell type is uniformity. This attribute invites the following questions: is uniformity important and if it is why is size so uniform despite variation in growth rate and cell cycle length.

During my time at Sick Kids, I worked with Dr.Kafri and Dr.Ginzberg to unravel the mystery of cell size uniformity. Through experimentation and statistical analysis we were able to identify an inverse correlation between G1 length and growth rate. This paper discusses the ability of animal cells to autonomously sense their size and make corrections to prevent cell size from large aberrations.
